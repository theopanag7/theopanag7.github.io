Lattice Mesh Generation

6 December 2017 / PROJECTS

The goal of this project was to generate a custom 3D lattice support structure in between two arbitrary surfaces. These single-mesh lattice structures have a variety of uses in the medical field. They can be 3D printed and inserted into the human body to support a vain or a damaged organ.

Here's the process we followed given two arbitrary planar point clouds:

  • We first triangulate (Delaunay) the two point clouds and generate two parallel planar surfaces.
  • We then implement Delaunay Tetrahedralization to fill the space in between the two planes with tetrahedra primitives.
  • We use the edges of the tetrahedra as support beams for our lattice.
  • Finally, we use the ball-pivoting algorithm to generate a single 3D mesh from all the primitives. This step is crucial since it allows 3D printing.

Project completed by me and Sandeep Bangalore at Georgia Tech, Atlanta.
