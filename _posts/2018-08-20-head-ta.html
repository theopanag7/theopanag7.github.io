During Fall 2018, I work as a Head Teaching Assistant for Georgia Tech's graduate-level Artificial Intelligence course (CS6601).

I work closely with Dr. Thad Starner, inventor of Google Glass and Symbiotic AI pioneer, helping him prepare, run and improve the course. I lead a team of 13 Graduate Teaching Assistants and I am responsible for 590 graduate students (140 on-campus, 450 online).

I started as a Graduate Teaching Assistant in Spring 2018, and became a Head TA a semester later.

Responsiblities

  • Creation of the Midterm and Final exam questions. The exams are around 30-40pp, are take-home, and test various different aspects of AI.
  • Development and maintenance of Python-based AI projects for the students. The projects are hosted on the cloud, are auto-graded and span various different subjects.
  • Coordination of student communications on various online platforms (Piazza, Slack, Google Hangouts etc.)
  • Substitute Teaching a class of 100+ students on AI research topics.
  • Updating course content with recent AI research papers, articles and videos.

Subjects


- Game Playing
- Search
- Simulated Annealing
- Constraint Satisfaction Problems
- Probability
- Bayes Nets
- Machine Learning (Clustering, Decision Trees, Neural Nets, Deep Learning)
- Pattern Recognition through Time (DTW, HMMs)
- Logic and Planning
- Planning under Uncertainty
